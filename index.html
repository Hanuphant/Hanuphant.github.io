About Me

I'm a passionate Bioinformatics Analyst specializing in Statistical Genomics, Multi-Omics analysis, and computational biology. Currently working as a Bioinformatics Analyst at Geisinger, I specialize in GWAS analysis, RNA-Seq processing, and developing automated bioinformatics pipelines.

With an M.S. in Bioinformatics from Georgia Institute of Technology and extensive experience in multi-omic statistical genetics, I'm dedicated to advancing precision medicine through data-driven insights and innovative computational approaches.

Work Experience

Implementing large-scale genomic analyses and developing bioinformatics pipelines for precision medicine initiatives.

  • Performed GWAS for obesity and anthropomorphic traits on 230k WES samples
  • Executed post-GWAS analyses: conditional analysis, fine mapping, colocalization, gene enrichment
  • Analyzed liver adipose tissue RNA-Seq for cis-QTL mapping across 600 loci
  • Extracted multi-phenotype EHR data and collaborated with GIANT, CHARGE, ORAcLE consortia

Conducted research in population genetics and biomarker analysis using large-scale genomic datasets.

  • Correlated Biological Health Score with genetic variation in UKBB full-sibling cohort
  • Computed kinship estimates and evaluated multivariate statistics on biomarker changes

Developed computational pipelines for drug discovery and molecular analysis in biotechnology startup environment.

  • Processed DEL NGS data with optimized Levenshtein algorithm pipelines
  • Identified top molecular compounds via enrichment and clustering analyses
  • Automated conservation scorecards and ligand discovery workflows using Python
